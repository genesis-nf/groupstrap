Newsweek's Future of Travel Awards 2021

The world is slowly opening up again, but travel will never be the same. While it's still uncertain how the pandemic will develop in the days ahead, vaccines are rolling out along with vaccine requirements needed to cross borders. So much is unknown, but one thing is certain: Travelers are ready to see the world they have been missing for so long.

After being isolated and grounded during the pandemic, we will never take the privilege of traveling for granted again. Tourism is also one of the main sources of income for many countries, and the negative economic impact of fewer travelers has been felt everywhere. The confluence of the pandemic, the raised consciousness from the Black Lives Matter movement and the climate crisis makes it even more urgent to reassess travel and its impact on people and the planet.

Newsweek's Future of Travel Awards highlights those who are creating a travel industry for the future—one that's more adaptable, sustainable, responsible, innovative and inclusive. The COVID-19 crisis has had a detrimental impact on the tourism industry, but it is also giving an opportunity for the travel space to rethink and reimagine what effect it could have on the world. So we are recognizing companies and individuals who are rebuilding a travel industry for a brighter and more resilient future.

We combed through hundreds of nominees for Newsweek's Future of Travel Awards. There's no blueprint for how travel will look post-pandemic. But we're hopeful the ability of travel to bring us together again will continue on. We've seen there's no shortage of people and companies focused on travel being a force for good—to positively impact the people and places it touches.

The finalists and winners for Newsweek's Future of Travel Awards were determined by our panel of advisers, all experts in different categories of travel.

Meet the Panelists

Natalia Bayona

Director of Innovation, Education and Investments, United Nations World Tourism Organization

Natalia Bayona leads the innovation, education and investments strategy of the World Tourism Organization (UNWTO), the United Nations specialized agency for the promotion of responsible, sustainable and accessible tourism worldwide. With a diverse background in politics, destination management and business, Bayona has become the first innovation director at UNWTO, has introduced entrepreneurship and education as key factors for tourism and development, and has positioned UNWTO as an innovation benchmark within the wider United Nations system, as recognized in the U.N. 2020 Mapping of Innovation, Data and Digital Capacities.

During the last three years, Bayona has developed seven UNWTO Tourism Startup Competitions and six Innovation Challenges with outcomes including more than $74 million in support for entrepreneurs and pilots for safe travel. In addition, Bayona has designed the UNWTO Tourism Tech Adventures innovation forums and co-created the UNWTO Tourism Online Academy.

Shannon Stowell

CEO of Adventure Travel Trade Association

Shannon Stowell leads the Adventure Travel Trade Associate (ATTA) as CEO after restarting the organization in 2004 to pursue the vision that ATTA could be a powerful force for good in the tourism industry.

For over a decade, Stowell has been advising and speaking around the world, advocating for responsible adventure tourism. He has served as a judge for numerous sustainability awards and programs, and emceed ITB Berlin's Corporate Social Responsibility day for two years.

Currently, Stowell serves on the National Advisory Council for National Blacks in Travel and Tourism Collaborative, and the advisory boards for Visit Seattle; U.S. Wildlife Trafficking Alliance; Tourism Cares; and the Adventure Travel Conservation Fund, which he co-founded. He also has been on the boards of both the Global Sustainable Tourism Council Board and the American Hiking Society.

Stephanie M. Jones

Founder and CEO, National Blacks in Travel and Tourism Collaborative

Stephanie M. Jones is the founder of some of the most innovative tourism initiatives to create diversity, equity and inclusion in the travel and tourism industry, such as the Cultural Heritage Economic Alliance Inc. (CHEA); Culture onShore, the world's first global marketplace for cultural heritage tours and activities; and National Blacks in Travel and Tourism Collaborative, an initiative to level the playing field for small Black and Brown travel and tourism businesses.

Jones is the co-founder for the Cultural and Heritage Tourism Summit and serves on the U.S. Travel and Tourism Advisory Board, Destinations International's Global Leadership, Governance and Nominating committees, and the Future of Tourism's advisory committee.

Zach Honig

Editor-at-Large at The Points Guy

Zach Honig is editor-at-large for The Points Guy (TPG), where he covers the aviation industry; airline and hotel loyalty programs; and more. Honig also produces TPG's daily newsletter and serves as host of the site's aviation-focused video series, Airplane Mode.

After a stint working at the 2008 Beijing Olympics, Honig covered the technology industry. At PC Magazine, he coordinated the publication's in-depth reviews coverage and produced a growing tech-focused podcast before moving to Engadget, where he served as the site's deputy managing editor.

Honig joined The Points Guy as the site's first editor in chief in 2015, helping to grow TPG from a staff of five to the global team you'll find today.

Honig, a lifelong aviation enthusiast, is an aerial photographer, and a licensed private aircraft and commercial drone pilot with an ever-growing passion for private and commercial air travel.

Paula Vlamings

Chief Impact Officer, Tourism Cares; Co-founder, Future of Tourism

For more than a decade, Paula Vlamings has worked at the intersection of tourism and sustainability. She is currently the chief impact officer at Tourism Cares, a nonprofit organization dedicated to advancing the positive impact of travel for people and the planet. She is also the co-founder of Future of Tourism, a coalition of six global tourism NGOs​ with the mission to place destinations​ ​at the center of tourism's recovery.

Prior to Tourism Cares, Vlamings was the executive director of Planeterra (the nonprofit foundation of the global travel company G Adventures), where she pioneered incubating social enterprises supporting women, youth and indigenous communities in the tourism supply chain.

In 2014, Vlamings spent two years on the road driving from San Francisco to Tierra del Fuego, Argentina, and across 17 countries, chronicled on her site OurBiggerPicture.

Oneika Raymond

Travel Journalist and On-Air TV Host

Oneika Raymond is an Emmy-nominated correspondent for NBC New York and host of Travel Channel's digital series Big City, Little Budget and One Bag and You're Out. She appears regularly as a travel expert on television networks including CNN, CTV and CGTN. She also is the host and co-producer of About the Journey, a podcast from Marriott Bonvoy Traveler, and has been a guest host on NBC's Emmy Award–winning travel series 1st Look.

Raymond has contributed to Condé Nast Traveler, O magazine, Ebony magazine, and Lonely Planet. She is the voice behind the site Oneika the Traveller, where she examines travel through the intersections of race, politics and privilege. In 2018, the site was awarded a Gold Medal for Best Travel Blog by the Society of American Travel Writers. Follow her adventures on Instagram @oneikatraveller.

Norie Quintos

Contributing Editor at National Geographic Travel

An award-winning freelance journalist, Norie Quintos is a contributing editor for National Geographic Travel Media and a communications consultant for the travel industry. She has spoken and presented at conferences including the Adventure Travel World Summit, The New York Times Travel Show, the International Indigenous Tourism Conference, and the World Bank World Tourism Day Forum.

Quintos is a member of the board of directors of the Adventure Travel Conservation Fund and an adviser to Adventure Media, the Transformational Travel Council, Impact Travel Alliance and the social enterprise startup GivingWay.

She is the former executive editor of National Geographic Traveler and was an editor at U.S. News and World Report and Caribbean Travel and Life. Follow her on Twitter @noriecicerone.

Jamie Sweeting

President, Planeterra

Jamie Sweeting is the president of Planeterra and has spent the past 25 years working in tourism, conservation, development and business management. In his decades of traversing the globe, he has yet to encounter another organization like Planeterra that is as effective at utilizing tourism to improve the lives of some of the most under-resourced people on this planet—especially marginalized women, disadvantaged youth and rural and indigenous communities.

Prior to Planeterra, Sweeting was global chief environmental officer at Royal Caribbean Cruises Ltd. and headed up the travel industry program at the Center for Environmental Leadership in Business at Conservation International. He was formerly chairman of Sustainable Travel International and has served on the boards of the International Ecotourism Society and the Global Sustainable Tourism Council, among others.

Kathleen Rellihan

Travel Editor at Newsweek

Kathleen Rellihan is the travel editor for Newsweek and covers sustainable travel, culture and adventure. For Newsweek, she has interviewed Jane Goodall on her hopes for the planet, actor Ewan McGregor about riding 13,000 miles through Latin America on an electric Harley,  and Hearts in the Ice, the first women to overwinter in the Arctic alone for climate action.

Prior to Newsweek, Rellihan helped relaunch Adventure.com as editor-in-chief, and worked at Travel Channel as a digital editor. She has contributed to AFAR, CNN Travel, Condé Nast Traveler, Departures, Architectural Digest, Hemispheres, Fodor's, Travel and Leisure, and more.

Rellihan is a media herald for the Transformational Travel Council and a member of Women Travel Leaders. She co-founded the Washington, D.C., Travel Massive, and since 2013, she's been an ambassador for EIMHaiti, a Haitian adult language school. Follow her on Twitter @k_rellihan.
